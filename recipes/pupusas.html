Pupusas - Recipe

Description

Pupusas are a thick griddle cake or flatbread from El Salvador. It has been declared the national dish of El Salvador. Pupusas are usually stuffed with one or more ingredients, which may include cheese, loroco, chicharr√≥n, squash, or refried beans. It is typically accompanied by curtido, a spicy fermented cabbage slaw and/or tomato salsa. It is traditionally eaten by hand, NO UTENSILS!

Ingredients

  • beans (cooked)
  • onions
  • corn oil
  • salt
  • mozzarella cheese
  • green bell pepper
  • chile
  • masa corn flour

Steps

  1. Use corn oil in large soup pan
  2. Cook onions and beans on pan
  3. Shred the mozzarella cheese with lorocco and bell pepper
  4. Place cheese mixture into plastic bowl in microwave
  5. Create masa mix using water
  6. Make an egg size of masa in your hand and fill it with cheese
  7. Flatten it with your palms to form a thick disc
  8. Place pupusa on non stick surface pan and cook until golden brown
  9. Serve and enjoy!
